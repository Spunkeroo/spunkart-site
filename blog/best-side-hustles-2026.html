Freelancing (Hustles 1-4)

Freelancing remains the fastest way to turn existing skills into side income. The global freelance market reached $1.5 trillion in 2024 according to Statista, and platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and Toptal have made it trivially easy to find clients without leaving your house. The barrier to entry is your current skill set. The barrier to scaling is your ability to specialize and raise rates over time.

1. Freelance Writing & Copywriting

Businesses need blog posts, landing page copy, email sequences, product descriptions, white papers, and social media content. Despite AI writing tools, demand for skilled human writers has actually increased because companies need someone who can direct AI output, fact-check it, and add brand voice. The Writers Guild 2025 income survey found that freelance content writers averaged $4,200 per month, with SEO-focused writers averaging $5,800. Niching down is the key: a writer who specializes in B2B SaaS or healthcare earns double what a generalist does.

Monthly Income: $1,500 - $8,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $50 Time Commitment: 10 - 25 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: Google Docs (free), Grammarly (free tier), Hemingway Editor (free), Contently or Upwork (free to join) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 3 weeks
2. Freelance Graphic Design

Every business needs visual identity: logos, social media graphics, pitch decks, brand kits, packaging design, and ad creatives. Canva democratized basic design, but that also raised the bar for what "professional" looks like. Skilled designers who understand brand strategy, typography, and conversion-focused design command premium rates. According to the AIGA 2025 salary survey, freelance designers averaged $75 per hour, with brand identity specialists hitting $125 or more. Platforms like 99designs and Dribbble are good starting points, but direct outreach to agencies and startups pays better.

Monthly Income: $2,000 - $10,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $60/month Time Commitment: 10 - 30 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium Tools: Canva (free tier), Figma (free tier), GIMP (free), Photopea (free), Dribbble (free portfolio) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 4 weeks
3. Freelance Web Development

The demand for web developers has not slowed despite no-code tools. If anything, no-code created more demand because businesses that started on Wix or Squarespace eventually outgrow those platforms and need custom work. The Stack Overflow 2025 Developer Survey reported that freelance web developers earned a median of $85 per hour in the US, with full-stack developers averaging $95. Specializing in a framework (Next.js, Laravel, WordPress) or an industry (e-commerce, healthcare, legal) lets you charge 40% to 60% more than generalists. Client acquisition is straightforward: build three portfolio sites, publish them on GitHub, and start reaching out to agencies that overflow work.

Monthly Income: $3,000 - $15,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $20 Time Commitment: 10 - 30 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium to High Tools: VS Code (free), GitHub (free), Vercel (free tier), Netlify (free tier), SpunkArt free dev tools Time to First Dollar: 2 - 6 weeks
4. Freelance Video Editing

Short-form video dominates every platform in 2026. YouTube, TikTok, Instagram Reels, and LinkedIn video all need edited content, and most creators and businesses do not have the time or skill to edit themselves. A Fiverr market report from late 2025 showed that video editing was the third-most-requested freelance category, with average project fees between $150 and $500 for short-form and $500 to $2,000 for long-form. The learning curve is real but manageable. Start with DaVinci Resolve (completely free and professional-grade), edit 10 videos for free or cheap to build a reel, then start charging. Creators with consistent upload schedules make the best recurring clients.

Monthly Income: $1,000 - $7,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $100 Time Commitment: 10 - 25 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium Tools: DaVinci Resolve (free), CapCut (free), Canva Video (free tier), Pexels/Pixabay (free stock) Time to First Dollar: 2 - 4 weeks

Digital Products & Reselling (Hustles 5-8)

Digital products are the closest thing to true passive income a side hustler can build. You create once, sell infinitely, and there is no inventory, shipping, or marginal cost per unit. The global digital goods market hit $331 billion in 2025 according to Grand View Research. Whether you create from scratch or resell existing products with white-label rights, the economics are compelling: 80% to 95% profit margins after platform fees.

5. Create and Sell Digital Templates

Notion templates, Canva templates, spreadsheet templates, resume templates, social media template packs, website themes. The template economy exploded in 2024 and 2025 and shows no signs of slowing. Easlo, a solo Notion template creator, publicly reported crossing $500,000 in total revenue in 2025. You do not need to be at that level -- a well-designed Notion template pack or Canva bundle on Gumroad can earn $500 to $3,000 per month with the right niche targeting. The key is solving a specific problem: "Social Media Calendar for Realtors" sells ten times better than "Social Media Calendar." You do not need to create products from scratch. White-label and resell rights let you take existing digital products, rebrand them, and sell them as your own. This includes tool bundles, ebook collections, template packs, course materials, and software utilities. The profit margins are enormous because your only cost is the one-time license fee. 7. Sell Online Courses and Workshops

The e-learning market is projected to reach $462 billion globally by 2027 (Research and Markets, 2025). If you have expertise in anything -- Excel, photography, cooking, guitar, fitness, marketing, coding -- someone will pay to learn it from you. You do not need a production studio. A Loom screen recording, a slide deck, and a clear outline are enough to launch a $49 to $199 course. Platforms like Teachable, Gumroad, and Udemy handle payments and hosting. The 2025 Course Creator Income Report from Teachable showed that creators with a single course earned a median of $1,200 per month, while those with three or more courses averaged $4,500 per month.

8. Sell Printables on Etsy

Printables are digital files (planners, wall art, checklists, worksheets, stickers, invitations) that customers download and print themselves. The overhead is essentially zero: no inventory, no shipping, no returns. Etsy remains the dominant marketplace, with over 96 million active buyers as of 2025. Top printable sellers report $2,000 to $10,000 per month, though the realistic range for someone starting out is $200 to $2,000 within three to six months. The key is volume and SEO: list 50 to 100 products, optimize every title and tag for Etsy search, and reinvest early profits into promoted listings. Canva's free tier is all you need to design professional printables.

Content Creation (Hustles 9-12)

Content creation is no longer just a hobby. The creator economy hit $250 billion in 2025 according to Goldman Sachs, and it is one of the most accessible paths to side income because every platform is actively paying creators to keep users engaged. You do not need millions of followers. Micro-creators with 1,000 to 50,000 followers across YouTube, TikTok, newsletters, and podcasts are earning significant money through ads, sponsorships, affiliate revenue, and digital product sales.

9. YouTube Channel (Long-Form and Shorts)

YouTube remains the highest-paying content platform per view. The YouTube Partner Program pays between $3 and $12 per 1,000 views depending on niche, with finance, tech, and business channels at the top. A channel with 10,000 subscribers producing two videos per week can realistically earn $1,000 to $4,000 per month from AdSense alone, before sponsorships. YouTube Shorts now also monetize, paying $0.04 to $0.08 per 1,000 views through the revenue sharing model introduced in 2023. The compounding effect is the real advantage: videos you publish today will still generate views and revenue two to three years from now. Start with what you know, be consistent, and optimize thumbnails obsessively.

Monthly Income: $500 - $10,000+ (after monetization) Startup Cost: $0 - $200 Time Commitment: 10 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium to High Tools: Smartphone camera (free), DaVinci Resolve (free), Canva for thumbnails (free), TubeBuddy (free tier) Time to First Dollar: 3 - 6 months (1,000 subs + 4,000 hrs needed)
10. Newsletter / Substack

Email newsletters are having a renaissance. Substack alone has over 35 million active subscriptions as of 2025, and the platform makes it dead simple to monetize with paid tiers. But even free newsletters make money through sponsorships and affiliate links. A newsletter with 5,000 free subscribers can charge $200 to $500 per sponsored slot, and if you publish weekly, that is $800 to $2,000 per month from sponsorships alone. The economics get better as you grow: newsletters with 20,000+ subscribers command $1,000 to $3,000 per sponsor. Start by picking a specific niche (AI tools, indie hacking, personal finance, crypto), curate valuable content, and publish on a consistent schedule. Building an audience through your newsletter also feeds every other side hustle on this list.

Monthly Income: $300 - $5,000+ Startup Cost: $0 Time Commitment: 3 - 8 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: Substack (free), Beehiiv (free tier), ConvertKit (free up to 1,000 subs), Canva (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 3 months

Affiliate marketing is earning a commission by promoting other companies' products. In 2026, it is a $17 billion global industry (Influencer Marketing Hub data). The side hustlers who succeed at this are the ones who build content around products they genuinely use and recommend. A blog post reviewing "Best Project Management Tools for Freelancers" with affiliate links can earn $200 to $1,000 per month if it ranks on Google. A YouTube video comparing two popular SaaS tools can earn even more. Commission rates range from 5% to 50% depending on the product category, with software and digital products typically paying 20% to 40% recurring. The key is SEO: rank for buyer-intent keywords and let the traffic convert on autopilot. Check our 50 marketing tips on zero budget for strategies to drive traffic without spending a dime.

Monthly Income: $200 - $8,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $50 (domain + hosting) Time Commitment: 8 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium Tools: WordPress (free), Google Search Console (free), Ahrefs free webmaster tools, Amazon Associates (free), ShareASale (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 3 months
12. Podcasting

Podcasting has matured from a hobby into a legitimate revenue channel. Edison Research reports that 42% of Americans listen to podcasts monthly in 2025, up from 38% in 2023. Monetization happens through sponsorships (typically $18 to $50 per 1,000 downloads), listener support (Patreon, Buy Me a Coffee), premium episodes, and selling your own products or services. A podcast with 1,000 downloads per episode can earn $500 to $1,500 per month through a combination of these channels. The startup costs are nearly zero: your phone microphone works for the first 20 episodes. Upgrade to a $60 USB mic when you have proven you can stick with the schedule. The best podcast niches for monetization in 2026 are business, finance, tech, health, and true crime.

E-Commerce & Dropshipping (Hustles 13-16)

E-commerce continues to grow at 8% to 10% annually, and the tools available to solo sellers in 2026 make it possible to run a legitimate online store with zero inventory and minimal upfront investment. The models range from dropshipping (you never touch the product) to print-on-demand (custom designs on physical products) to Amazon FBA (you ship inventory to Amazon and they handle fulfillment). Each has different tradeoffs between control, margins, and effort.

13. Print-on-Demand (Merch, Apparel, Accessories)

Print-on-demand lets you sell custom-designed products (t-shirts, mugs, phone cases, posters, tote bags) without holding any inventory. When someone buys, the printing partner produces and ships the item directly. Your only job is creating designs and marketing. Platforms like Printful, Printify, and Merch by Amazon handle the rest. According to Grand View Research, the print-on-demand market reached $8.6 billion in 2025. Realistic income for someone putting in consistent effort is $500 to $3,000 per month within six months, with top sellers earning $10,000 or more. Success hinges on design quality and niche targeting: "Funny Nurse Shirts" outperforms "Cool T-Shirts" every time.

Monthly Income: $300 - $5,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $30 Time Commitment: 5 - 15 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: Canva (free), Printful (free to start), Printify (free), Etsy or Shopify ($1/month trial), Kittl (free tier) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 4 weeks
14. Dropshipping With a Niche Focus

Dropshipping's reputation took a hit from get-rich-quick grifters, but the business model itself is sound when done properly. You list products from suppliers on your own branded store, and when someone orders, the supplier ships directly to them. The difference between the price you charge and the price you pay is your profit. The key change in 2026 is that generic stores selling random products are dead. What works now is niche stores with curated products, strong branding, and content marketing. A store focused on "eco-friendly pet products" or "minimalist desk accessories" with 15 to 30 well-chosen products outperforms a store with 500 random items. AutoDS and DSers remain the most popular automation tools. Average margins run 15% to 40% depending on the niche and supplier.

Monthly Income: $500 - $8,000+ Startup Cost: $30 - $200 Time Commitment: 10 - 25 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium Tools: Shopify ($1/month for 3 months), DSers (free), CJ Dropshipping (free), Canva (free), Google Analytics (free) Time to First Dollar: 2 - 6 weeks
15. Amazon FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon)

Amazon FBA lets you send products to Amazon's warehouses, and they handle storage, packing, shipping, and customer service. You focus on finding products, optimizing listings, and driving sales. The 2025 Jungle Scout State of the Amazon Seller report found that 57% of Amazon FBA sellers earn over $1,000 per month in profit, and 20% earn over $5,000. The startup cost is higher than other side hustles because you need to purchase inventory upfront, but the advantage is access to Amazon's 300+ million active customers. Private label (sourcing a generic product, adding your brand, and selling it as your own) is the most profitable FBA model. Start with a product under $15 wholesale that you can sell for $25 to $40 retail.

Monthly Income: $1,000 - $10,000+ Startup Cost: $500 - $3,000 Time Commitment: 10 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium to High Tools: Jungle Scout ($49/mo), Helium 10 (free tier), Amazon Seller Central (free), Canva for listings (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 3 months
16. Sell on Facebook Marketplace & Poshmark (Flipping)

Flipping -- buying items below market value and reselling them for a profit -- is one of the oldest side hustles and it works better than ever in 2026 because online platforms have eliminated geographic limitations. You can source from thrift stores, garage sales, estate sales, clearance racks, and liquidation pallets, then resell on Facebook Marketplace, Poshmark, eBay, or Mercari. The average reseller on Poshmark earned $1,146 per month in 2025 according to the Poshmark Seller Report, with top sellers (Posh Ambassadors) averaging over $3,000. Categories with the best margins include electronics, brand-name clothing, vintage items, books, and collectibles. Start with what you already know -- if you can spot a good deal on sneakers, furniture, or vintage clothing, you already have the core skill.

Monthly Income: $500 - $5,000+ Startup Cost: $50 - $500 Time Commitment: 8 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: Facebook Marketplace (free), Poshmark (free), eBay (free), smartphone camera (free), Google Lens for price checks (free) Time to First Dollar: Same week

Tutoring & Coaching (Hustles 17-19)

Knowledge-based side hustles have the highest margins because your product is expertise, and expertise costs nothing to deliver once you have it. The global online tutoring market reached $20.6 billion in 2025 (Grand View Research), and the coaching industry hit $4.5 billion. These numbers keep growing because people increasingly prefer flexible, one-on-one learning over traditional classroom settings.

17. Online Tutoring

If you are competent in any academic subject, standardized test prep (SAT, GRE, GMAT), or language, online tutoring is one of the most reliable side hustles available. Platforms like Wyzant, Tutor.com, and Preply connect tutors with students, and rates range from $25 to $80 per hour for standard subjects and $80 to $200 per hour for test prep and specialized topics. The Wyzant 2025 Tutor Earnings Report showed that active tutors (15+ hours per week) earned a median of $3,400 per month. You do not need a teaching degree -- most platforms only require demonstrated knowledge and a background check. Building direct clients through word of mouth and social media eliminates platform fees (typically 20% to 25%) and lets you keep the full rate.

Monthly Income: $1,000 - $6,000+ Startup Cost: $0 Time Commitment: 5 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: Zoom (free tier), Google Meet (free), Wyzant (free to join), Preply (free to join), Google Docs (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 2 weeks
18. Business or Career Coaching

Coaching is selling transformation, not information. A business coach helps entrepreneurs grow revenue. A career coach helps professionals land better jobs or negotiate raises. A productivity coach helps people reclaim their time. The International Coaching Federation's 2025 Global Coaching Study reported that the average coach earned $62,000 per year, but those specializing in executive or business coaching averaged $120,000+. As a side hustle, coaching four to six clients at $200 to $500 per session, meeting weekly or biweekly, generates $2,000 to $6,000 per month on 8 to 12 hours of actual coaching time. Start by coaching three people for free to build testimonials, then raise prices with every new client.

Monthly Income: $1,500 - $8,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $50 Time Commitment: 5 - 15 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium Tools: Zoom (free), Calendly (free tier), Notion for client notes (free), Stripe for payments (free to start) Time to First Dollar: 2 - 4 weeks
19. Paid Community / Membership

A paid community is a group of people who pay a monthly fee for access to exclusive content, direct access to you, networking with peers, and accountability. Think of it as coaching at scale. Instead of charging one person $300 per month, you charge 50 people $29 per month for a group experience. That is $1,450 per month and it scales without proportionally increasing your time. Discord, Circle, and Skool are the leading platforms for hosting paid communities in 2026. Skool reported that its top 1,000 communities averaged $8,200 per month in revenue in 2025. The formula is simple: build an audience around a specific topic, prove your expertise with free content, then offer deeper access behind a paywall.

Monthly Income: $500 - $10,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $99/month Time Commitment: 5 - 10 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium Tools: Discord (free), Skool ($99/mo), Circle (free trial), Stripe (free to start), Canva (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 3 months

App & Tool Building (Hustles 20-22)

If you can code -- even at a beginner level -- building small apps and tools is one of the highest-ceiling side hustles in 2026. The micro-SaaS movement has proven that a single developer can build, launch, and maintain a product that generates $1,000 to $50,000 per month. You do not need a revolutionary idea. You need a boring problem that a specific group of people will pay monthly to have solved.

20. Micro-SaaS (Small Software Products)

Micro-SaaS products are small, focused software tools that solve one specific problem for a niche audience. Think: a Chrome extension that auto-formats LinkedIn posts, a Slack bot that tracks team standups, or a browser tool that monitors competitor pricing. The advantages over traditional SaaS are lower development costs, faster time to market, and the ability to run them as a one-person operation. The IndieHackers revenue database shows hundreds of solo-built micro-SaaS products earning between $1,000 and $20,000 MRR. The median time from idea to first paying customer is about six weeks for experienced developers and three to four months for beginners. Start by identifying a workflow you or people around you do repeatedly that could be automated with a simple tool.

Monthly Income: $500 - $20,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $50 Time Commitment: 10 - 25 hrs/week Difficulty: High Tools: VS Code (free), Vercel (free tier), Supabase (free tier), Stripe (free to start), GitHub (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 4 months
21. Browser Extensions and Plugins

Browser extensions occupy a unique sweet spot: they are relatively simple to build, easy to distribute through the Chrome Web Store and Firefox Add-ons, and can be monetized through freemium models, one-time purchases, or subscriptions. The Chrome Web Store has over 200,000 extensions, but most niches are still underserved. Extensions that save time on repetitive tasks (auto-filling forms, batch downloading images, formatting text) consistently perform well. Development typically takes two to eight weeks for a useful v1, and the distribution is essentially free since the browser stores act as discovery platforms. Several solo developers have publicly shared that their Chrome extensions earn $2,000 to $10,000 per month through a combination of premium tiers and one-time payments.

Monthly Income: $300 - $10,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $25 (Chrome Web Store registration: $5) Time Commitment: 8 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Medium to High Tools: VS Code (free), Chrome DevTools (free), GitHub (free), Stripe (free to start) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 3 months
22. No-Code App Building

You do not need to know how to code to build and sell software in 2026. No-code platforms like Bubble, Glide, Softr, and FlutterFlow let you build functional web and mobile apps using visual editors. The no-code market is projected to reach $65 billion by 2027 (Gartner, 2025). Realistic use cases include building internal tools for small businesses ($500 to $5,000 per project), creating marketplace or directory apps, building booking or scheduling systems, and creating workflow automation tools. You can also build no-code apps as products: a membership directory, a niche job board, or a local event listing app. Charge monthly subscriptions or per-transaction fees. The development time is a fraction of traditional coding, typically one to four weeks for a functional MVP.

Monthly Income: $500 - $8,000+ Startup Cost: $0 - $30/month Time Commitment: 8 - 20 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: Bubble (free tier), Glide (free tier), Softr (free tier), Airtable (free tier), Stripe (free to start) Time to First Dollar: 2 - 8 weeks

Crypto Opportunities (Hustles 23-24)

The crypto ecosystem in 2026 offers legitimate side income opportunities that go beyond speculative trading. The industry has matured significantly since the 2021 hype cycle, and the projects that survived are building real products with real users. If you understand the technology or are willing to learn, there are ways to earn that do not require risking your savings on price speculation.

23. Crypto Airdrops, Testnets, and Bounties

Crypto projects regularly distribute free tokens to early users through airdrops, testnet participation, and bug bounties. The Arbitrum airdrop in 2023 gave qualifying wallets $1,500 to $10,000+ worth of tokens. The LayerZero, zkSync, and Starknet airdrops in 2024 and 2025 continued the trend, with active testnet users receiving between $500 and $5,000 on average. The strategy is straightforward: identify new protocols before they launch a token, use their testnet products, provide feedback, and participate in their communities. Platforms like Galxe, Layer3, and Zealy aggregate bounty and quest opportunities. This is not guaranteed income, but dedicated "airdrop farmers" who track five to ten protocols consistently report earning $2,000 to $10,000+ per year from airdrops alone. The time commitment is low -- usually 30 minutes to two hours per protocol per week.

Monthly Income: $0 - $5,000+ (highly variable, lump sum payouts) Startup Cost: $0 - $50 (gas fees) Time Commitment: 3 - 10 hrs/week Difficulty: Low to Medium Tools: MetaMask (free), Galxe (free), Layer3 (free), DeBank (free), Twitter/X for alpha (free) Time to First Dollar: 1 - 12 months (depends on airdrop timing)
24. Crypto Content Creation and Education

The crypto space has a massive knowledge gap. Millions of people want to understand blockchain, DeFi, NFTs, and Web3 but struggle with the technical jargon and rapidly changing landscape. Crypto YouTube channels earn among the highest CPMs on the platform ($15 to $40 per 1,000 views) because of the high-value advertisers in the space. Twitter/X crypto accounts with educational threads build followings fast because the audience is hungry for clear, unbiased information. How to Pick the Right Side Hustle for You

With 25 options on the table, the worst thing you can do is try three at once. The people who fail at side hustles are not picking the wrong hustle -- they are splitting their focus across too many. Here is a simple framework for choosing:

Step 1: Match Your Existing Skills

The fastest path to income is leveraging what you already know. If you can write, start with freelance writing or a newsletter. If you can design, start with templates or print-on-demand. If you can code, build a micro-SaaS or browser extension. Do not learn a brand new skill just to start a side hustle. Use the skills you have today and learn new ones as you earn.

Step 2: Match Your Available Time

Be honest about how many hours per week you can commit. If it is under five, go with digital product reselling or printables. If it is five to ten, go with tutoring, coaching, or a newsletter. If it is 15 or more, freelancing, YouTube, or building a micro-SaaS become viable. The worst outcome is starting something that requires 20 hours per week when you only have eight, burning out in month two, and quitting.

Step 3: Match Your Income Timeline

Some hustles pay immediately (freelancing, tutoring, flipping). Others take months to generate income (YouTube, SEO affiliate sites, micro-SaaS). If you need money within 30 days, skip the long-game plays and start with service-based hustles. If you have the runway to wait three to six months for income to start, the content and product-based hustles typically have higher long-term ceilings.

Step 4: Start One, Commit for 90 Days

Pick the single side hustle that best matches your skills, time, and timeline. Commit to it for 90 days before evaluating. Most side hustles need at least 60 to 90 days of consistent effort before you can accurately assess their potential. Quitting after three weeks because you have not made money yet is not a signal that the hustle does not work -- it is a signal that you did not give it enough time.

Income Stacking: Combine Hustles for Maximum Earnings

The real power of side hustles comes from stacking complementary income streams. Once your first hustle is generating consistent income (three or more months in a row), add a second one that leverages the same audience, skills, or content. Here are three proven stacking combinations:

Stack 1: Freelance Writing + Newsletter + Affiliate Marketing. Write freelance articles for clients to earn active income. Publish a weekly newsletter in the same niche to build an audience. Monetize the newsletter with affiliate links. Your freelance work feeds content ideas for the newsletter, and the newsletter builds authority that commands higher freelance rates.

Stack 2: Online Tutoring + Course Creation + Paid Community. Tutor students one-on-one for immediate income. Package your most common lessons into an online course for passive income. Create a paid community for ongoing support and accountability. Each layer builds on the previous one without requiring entirely new skills.

Stack 3: Digital Templates + Reselling + YouTube. Create and sell digital templates on Etsy and Gumroad. Add resold products to your store for additional passive revenue. Start a YouTube channel teaching people how to use the tools and templates you sell. The YouTube channel drives traffic to your store, and the store gives you content ideas for YouTube.
