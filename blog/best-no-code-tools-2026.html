Why No-Code in 2026

The no-code movement started as a way for non-technical founders to prototype ideas. In 2026, it has evolved into a legitimate way to build, launch, and scale entire businesses. Companies generating seven figures in annual revenue have been built entirely on no-code stacks. The tools are faster, more reliable, and more feature-rich than they were even two years ago.

Here is what changed. First, AI integration is now native to most no-code platforms. Bubble, Glide, and others have built-in AI features that let you add intelligent functionality without touching an API. Second, performance has caught up. The criticism that no-code apps were slow and clunky is largely outdated -- modern platforms compile to optimized code that performs well at scale. Third, the ecosystem has matured. There are now no-code solutions for every part of the business stack, and they integrate cleanly with each other.

The real question in 2026 is not whether no-code tools are capable enough. It is which combination of tools gives you the fastest path from idea to revenue. That is exactly what this guide answers.

Website Builders

Your website is the foundation of your online business. Whether you need a simple landing page, a full marketing site, or a complex web application, there is a no-code website builder that fits. The key is choosing the right level of complexity for what you actually need.

Carrd -- Best for Simple Landing Pages

Free plan available | Pro: $19/year

Carrd is the fastest way to get a one-page website live. If you need a landing page, a link-in-bio page, or a simple product page, Carrd does it in under an hour. The free plan lets you build three sites with a carrd.co subdomain. The Pro plan at $19 per year (not per month) unlocks custom domains, forms, payment widgets, and more sites. It is the most cost-effective website builder on the market by a wide margin.

  • Best for: Landing pages, waitlists, personal sites, link-in-bio pages
  • Limitations: Single-page only, no blog functionality, limited dynamic content
  • Learning curve: Minimal -- most people build their first site in 30 minutes

Framer -- Best for Modern Marketing Websites

Free plan available | Mini: $5/month | Basic: $15/month | Pro: $30/month

Framer has become the go-to tool for design-forward marketing sites. It combines a visual editor with powerful CMS, localization, and SEO features. The output is fast, responsive, and looks professional enough that many agencies now use it for client projects. Framer sites consistently score 90+ on Google PageSpeed, which matters for both SEO and conversion. The free plan is genuinely usable for testing and prototyping.

  • Best for: SaaS marketing sites, portfolios, agency sites, product launches
  • Limitations: Not ideal for e-commerce or complex web apps
  • Learning curve: Moderate -- design experience helps but is not required

Webflow -- Best for Full-Featured Business Websites

Free plan available | Basic: $18/month | CMS: $29/month | Business: $49/month

Webflow is the most powerful visual website builder available. It gives you the design freedom of custom code with the convenience of a visual editor. The CMS is robust enough for blogs, directories, job boards, and content-heavy sites. E-commerce is built in for those selling physical or digital products. The tradeoff is complexity -- Webflow has a steeper learning curve than Carrd or Framer, but the ceiling for what you can build is much higher.

  • Best for: Business websites, blogs, directories, e-commerce, client projects
  • Limitations: Pricing adds up with multiple sites, editor can feel overwhelming initially
  • Learning curve: Steeper -- plan for a week of learning before building confidently

WordPress + Elementor -- Best for Content-Heavy Sites

WordPress: Free (self-hosted) | Elementor Pro: $59/year

WordPress still powers over 40% of the internet, and for good reason. When combined with Elementor as a visual page builder, it becomes a no-code tool that handles virtually any use case. The plugin ecosystem is unmatched -- there are free plugins for SEO, caching, security, backups, forms, e-commerce, memberships, and thousands more. The downside is that WordPress requires hosting (typically $5 to $30 per month) and more maintenance than fully managed platforms.

  • Best for: Blogs, content sites, membership sites, WooCommerce stores
  • Limitations: Requires hosting and updates, security is your responsibility, can slow down with too many plugins
  • Learning curve: Moderate -- WordPress itself is intuitive but the ecosystem is vast

GitHub Pages -- Best for Free Static Sites

Completely free

If you know basic HTML and CSS -- or are willing to learn -- GitHub Pages lets you host unlimited static sites for free with custom domain support and HTTPS. It is what powers SpunkArt and dozens of other successful sites. Zero hosting costs, zero server management, automatic deployments on every push. For static marketing sites, landing pages, and documentation, it is hard to beat free. Read our complete GitHub Pages setup guide to get started.

  • Best for: Static sites, documentation, portfolios, blogs with Jekyll or Hugo
  • Limitations: No server-side processing, static content only
  • Learning curve: Low if you know HTML; requires learning Git basics

App Builders

Building a mobile or web app used to require a team of developers and months of work. In 2026, no-code app builders let a single person build, deploy, and iterate on fully functional applications. The category has split into two tiers: tools for building complex web apps with custom logic, and tools for building simpler mobile-first apps quickly.

Bubble -- Best for Complex Web Applications

Free plan available | Starter: $32/month | Growth: $134/month | Team: $414/month

Bubble is the most powerful no-code app builder on the market. You can build SaaS products, marketplaces, CRMs, project management tools, social networks, and virtually any web application you can imagine. It has a visual editor for the frontend, a workflow system for backend logic, a built-in database, user authentication, API connections, and plugin marketplace. Multiple companies built on Bubble have reached seven-figure revenue. The tradeoff is a significant learning curve -- expect to spend two to four weeks becoming proficient.

  • Best for: SaaS products, marketplaces, internal tools, complex web apps
  • Limitations: Performance can degrade with complex apps, vendor lock-in, steep learning curve
  • Learning curve: Steep -- Bubble has its own logic paradigm that takes time to master

Glide -- Best for Turning Spreadsheets Into Apps

Free plan available | Maker: $60/month | Team: $125/month

Glide takes a fundamentally different approach to app building: you start with a spreadsheet (Google Sheets or Airtable) and Glide automatically generates a mobile-friendly app from your data. This means if you can build a spreadsheet, you can build an app. It is particularly strong for internal business tools, directories, inventory trackers, and data-driven apps. The AI features added in 2025 let you add computed columns, classifications, and summaries powered by AI directly in your data layer.

  • Best for: Internal tools, directories, inventory apps, data-driven mobile apps
  • Limitations: Less flexible than Bubble for complex custom logic, tied to spreadsheet structure
  • Learning curve: Low -- if you know spreadsheets, you can build with Glide

Adalo -- Best for Native Mobile Apps

Free plan available | Starter: $45/month | Professional: $65/month | Team: $200/month

Adalo specializes in building native mobile apps that you can publish directly to the Apple App Store and Google Play. While Bubble and Glide create web apps that work on mobile browsers, Adalo creates actual native applications with push notifications, device features, and app store distribution. If your business specifically needs a presence in the app stores, Adalo is the most accessible path to get there without coding. The component marketplace lets you add advanced functionality like maps, charts, and integrations without building from scratch.

  • Best for: Native iOS and Android apps, consumer-facing mobile products
  • Limitations: Less powerful than Bubble for complex logic, app store review process still applies
  • Learning curve: Moderate -- intuitive editor but mobile-specific concepts take time

FlutterFlow -- Best for High-Performance Mobile Apps

Free plan available | Standard: $30/month | Pro: $70/month | Teams: $70/user/month

FlutterFlow builds on top of Google's Flutter framework, producing mobile apps that are noticeably faster and more polished than those from other no-code builders. The visual builder generates real Flutter code, which means you can export the code and continue development traditionally if you outgrow the no-code approach. It integrates natively with Firebase for the backend, making it a strong choice for apps that need real-time data, authentication, and cloud storage. The learning curve is steeper than Adalo but the output quality is higher.

  • Best for: High-performance mobile apps, apps that may need custom code later
  • Limitations: Requires understanding of app architecture concepts, Firebase costs can scale
  • Learning curve: Moderate to steep -- understanding data models is essential

Automation Tools

Automation is where no-code becomes genuinely transformative for solo founders and small teams. Instead of hiring people to handle repetitive tasks, you connect your tools together and let them run on autopilot. The time savings compound dramatically -- a single automation that saves you 15 minutes per day gives you back 91 hours per year. Read our 50 productivity tips for founders for more on maximizing your time.

Zapier -- Best for Simple Automations

Free plan: 100 tasks/month | Starter: $29.99/month | Professional: $73.50/month | Team: $103.50/month

Zapier connects over 7,000 apps and is the easiest automation tool to use. If you can describe your workflow as "when this happens in App A, do this in App B," Zapier handles it. Common automations include sending new email subscribers to a CRM, posting new blog articles to social media, creating invoices when orders come in, and alerting your team when reviews appear. The free plan gives you 100 tasks per month with single-step automations. Paid plans unlock multi-step workflows and more tasks.

  • Best for: Beginners, simple app-to-app connections, teams already using many SaaS tools
  • Limitations: Gets expensive at scale, limited logic capabilities, task-based pricing adds up
  • Learning curve: Very low -- most people create their first automation in minutes

Make (formerly Integromat) -- Best for Complex Workflows

Free plan: 1,000 ops/month | Core: $10.59/month | Pro: $18.82/month | Teams: $34.12/month

Make is more powerful and more affordable than Zapier for complex automations. Its visual workflow builder lets you create branching logic, loops, error handling, and multi-step processes that would require multiple Zapier automations. The pricing is based on operations rather than tasks, which means a 10-step automation counts as one scenario instead of 10 separate tasks. For anything beyond basic two-app connections, Make gives you significantly more capability per dollar.

  • Best for: Complex multi-step workflows, budget-conscious founders, data transformation
  • Limitations: Steeper learning curve than Zapier, fewer native integrations (1,800+ vs 7,000+)
  • Learning curve: Moderate -- the visual builder is intuitive but advanced features take time

n8n -- Best for Self-Hosted Automation

Self-hosted: Free (open source) | Cloud Starter: $24/month | Cloud Pro: $60/month

n8n is the open-source alternative to Zapier and Make. You can self-host it for free on your own server, giving you unlimited automations with no per-task pricing. The workflow builder is visual and supports complex logic including JavaScript code nodes for advanced transformations. For founders who are comfortable with basic server management, n8n eliminates automation costs entirely. The cloud version is available for those who prefer a managed experience.

  • Best for: Budget-maximizers, privacy-conscious businesses, developers who want extensibility
  • Limitations: Self-hosting requires server knowledge, smaller community than Zapier/Make
  • Learning curve: Moderate for cloud, higher for self-hosted setup

Databases & Backends

Every business application needs a place to store and manage data. No-code databases have evolved from simple spreadsheet alternatives into full backend platforms capable of powering real applications. The right choice depends on whether you need a standalone data management tool or a backend for a no-code app.

Airtable -- Best All-Around No-Code Database

Free plan: 1,000 records/base | Team: $24/user/month | Business: $54/user/month

Airtable looks like a spreadsheet but functions like a relational database. You can create linked records, roll-up calculations, automated workflows, and multiple views (grid, calendar, kanban, gallery, form) of the same data. It is the most popular no-code database for a reason: the interface is immediately familiar to anyone who has used Excel, but the underlying data model is far more powerful. Airtable works as a standalone tool for project management, CRM, content calendars, and inventory, or as the backend for Glide and other app builders.

  • Best for: CRM, project management, content planning, inventory, any structured data
  • Limitations: 1,000 record limit on free plan, per-user pricing gets expensive for teams
  • Learning curve: Low -- looks like a spreadsheet, acts like a database

Notion -- Best for Knowledge Management + Light Databases

Free plan available | Plus: $12/user/month | Business: $18/user/month

Notion combines documents, wikis, and databases into a single workspace. Its database feature is less powerful than Airtable for pure data management but more versatile as an all-in-one workspace. If your business needs a combination of documentation, SOPs, meeting notes, project tracking, and lightweight databases, Notion handles all of it in one tool. The free plan is generous for individual use, making it an excellent starting point for solo founders who need to organize everything in one place.

  • Best for: Internal wikis, SOPs, lightweight CRM, content planning, team documentation
  • Limitations: Database functionality is less robust than Airtable, slower with very large datasets
  • Learning curve: Low to moderate -- simple to start, deep functionality takes time to discover

Supabase -- Best for Developers Wanting a Real Backend

Free plan: 500 MB storage | Pro: $25/month | Team: $599/month

Supabase is an open-source Firebase alternative that provides a full Postgres database, authentication, file storage, edge functions, and real-time subscriptions. While it is more technical than Airtable or Notion, it bridges the gap between no-code and code beautifully. You get a real database with a web-based UI for managing data, and it integrates with no-code builders like Bubble, FlutterFlow, and Webflow through APIs. The free tier is generous enough to run a production app.

  • Best for: App backends, real-time features, authentication, projects that may scale
  • Limitations: More technical than pure no-code options, SQL knowledge helps
  • Learning curve: Moderate -- the dashboard is user-friendly but database concepts are essential

Google Sheets -- The Underrated Database

Free with a Google account

Do not underestimate Google Sheets as a no-code database. For early-stage businesses, a well-structured spreadsheet can serve as your CRM, inventory system, analytics dashboard, and more. It connects to Zapier, Make, Glide, and hundreds of other tools. The Google Sheets API lets you read and write data programmatically if needed. Many businesses generating six figures still run their operations on Google Sheets. Start simple, upgrade when you hit limitations.

  • Best for: Early-stage businesses, simple data needs, quick prototyping, Glide backends
  • Limitations: Not a real database, performance degrades beyond 10,000 rows, no relational features
  • Learning curve: None -- you already know how to use spreadsheets

Forms & Surveys

Forms are the connective tissue of most online businesses. Lead capture, customer onboarding, surveys, applications, order forms, feedback collection -- they all start with a form. The right form tool does more than collect responses; it routes data, triggers automations, and integrates with your stack.

Tally -- Best Free Form Builder

Free plan: Unlimited forms and submissions | Pro: $29/month

Tally is the best free form builder available in 2026. The free plan includes unlimited forms, unlimited submissions, and most features that competitors charge for. The editor works like a document -- you just type your questions and Tally automatically formats them into a clean, professional form. It supports conditional logic, calculations, file uploads, payment collection via Stripe, and integrations with Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets, and Slack. For most solo founders, the free plan is genuinely all you need.

  • Best for: Lead capture, surveys, applications, any form need on a budget
  • Limitations: Pro features include custom domains, team collaboration, and removing branding
  • Learning curve: Very low -- the simplest form builder to learn

Typeform -- Best for Conversational Forms

Free plan: 10 responses/month | Basic: $29/month | Plus: $59/month | Business: $99/month

Typeform pioneered the one-question-at-a-time format that dramatically increases completion rates. If your form is customer-facing and the experience matters -- think lead qualification, onboarding surveys, or product quizzes -- Typeform's conversational approach converts significantly better than traditional forms. The tradeoff is pricing: the free plan caps at 10 responses per month, and paid plans start at $29 per month. Worth it for high-value forms; overkill for simple contact forms.

  • Best for: Customer-facing forms where completion rate matters, quizzes, lead qualification
  • Limitations: Expensive for the number of responses, 10 response limit on free plan is very restrictive
  • Learning curve: Low -- beautiful editor that is intuitive to use

Google Forms -- Best for Quick Internal Forms

Completely free

Google Forms is not pretty, but it is free, fast, and sends responses directly to Google Sheets. For internal surveys, team feedback, simple registration forms, and anything where design is not the priority, it gets the job done in minutes. The automatic Charts integration gives you visual summaries of responses without any additional setup.

  • Best for: Internal forms, quick surveys, any situation where speed matters more than design
  • Limitations: Limited design customization, no payment collection, basic conditional logic
  • Learning curve: None

Payments & E-Commerce

Getting paid is the point. No-code payment and e-commerce tools let you sell digital products, physical goods, subscriptions, and services without building a custom checkout. If you are selling digital products specifically, read our detailed guide on how to make money selling digital products.

Stripe -- The Payment Infrastructure Standard

2.9% + 30 cents per transaction | No monthly fee

Stripe is not a no-code tool itself, but it powers the payments behind almost every no-code tool on this list. Stripe Payment Links let you create shareable checkout links with no code at all -- just set up a product, set the price, and share the link. For subscriptions, one-time payments, invoicing, and payment pages, Stripe's dashboard handles everything. It integrates natively with Webflow, Bubble, Carrd, Tally, and most other platforms on this list.

  • Best for: Any online payment need, subscriptions, invoicing, payment links
  • Limitations: The full power requires some technical knowledge, not ideal for physical product stores
  • Learning curve: Low for Payment Links, moderate for advanced features

Gumroad -- Best for Selling Digital Products

10% flat fee per transaction | No monthly fee

Gumroad is the simplest way to sell digital products: ebooks, templates, courses, software, music, art, and anything else that can be downloaded. You create a product page, set a price, and share the link. Gumroad handles checkout, delivery, taxes, and even affiliate programs. The 10% transaction fee is higher than Stripe's processing fees alone, but you get a complete storefront, audience features, and email capabilities included. For solo creators selling digital products, Gumroad removes every friction point between you and revenue.

  • Best for: Digital product sales, creators, ebooks, templates, courses
  • Limitations: 10% fee is high for high-volume sellers, limited customization of the storefront
  • Learning curve: Very low -- you can have a product live in under 10 minutes

Shopify -- Best for Full E-Commerce Stores

Basic: $39/month | Shopify: $105/month | Advanced: $399/month

If you are building a full online store with multiple products, inventory management, shipping, and customer accounts, Shopify is the standard for a reason. The theme system means you never touch code, the app store has solutions for every e-commerce need, and the checkout conversion rate is among the highest in the industry. Shopify also handles payments, taxes, shipping labels, and even point-of-sale for physical retail. It is more expensive than simpler alternatives but provides a complete e-commerce operating system.

  • Best for: Physical product stores, dropshipping, multi-product businesses, retail
  • Limitations: Monthly fees plus transaction fees add up, app costs can escalate quickly
  • Learning curve: Low to moderate -- basic stores are simple, advanced features take time

LemonSqueezy -- Best for Software and SaaS Sales

5% + 50 cents per transaction | No monthly fee

LemonSqueezy is built specifically for selling software, SaaS subscriptions, and digital products. It handles payments, tax compliance (including international VAT), license key generation, subscription management, and affiliate programs. The 5% fee is lower than Gumroad and you get features that matter for software sellers: license keys, software updates delivery, and usage-based billing. If you are building a SaaS product with no-code tools and need a way to sell subscriptions, LemonSqueezy is the cleanest solution.

  • Best for: SaaS products, software licenses, digital products, subscription businesses
  • Limitations: Smaller ecosystem than Stripe, fewer integrations than established platforms
  • Learning curve: Low -- the dashboard is modern and intuitive

Email Marketing

Email remains the highest-ROI marketing channel in 2026, generating roughly $36 for every $1 spent. Building an email list should start on day one of your business. These tools make it possible without writing a line of code. For a deeper dive, read our guide on how to build an email list from scratch.

Mailchimp -- Best for Beginners

Free plan: 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month | Essentials: $13/month | Standard: $20/month

Mailchimp is the most recognizable email marketing platform and the easiest to get started with. The free plan supports 500 contacts with 1,000 email sends per month, which is enough to start building and engaging an audience. The drag-and-drop email builder requires zero design skills, and the automation features let you set up welcome sequences, abandoned cart emails, and drip campaigns without coding. It also includes a basic landing page builder and simple analytics.

  • Best for: Absolute beginners, small lists under 500, simple newsletters and automations
  • Limitations: Gets expensive as your list grows, automation features are basic compared to competitors
  • Learning curve: Very low

ConvertKit (now Kit) -- Best for Creators

Free plan: 10,000 subscribers (limited features) | Creator: $29/month | Creator Pro: $59/month

Kit (formerly ConvertKit) is designed specifically for creators, bloggers, and solo founders. The free plan supports an impressive 10,000 subscribers, though with limited automation and no sequences. The paid plans unlock visual automation builders, subscriber tagging, A/B testing, and advanced segmentation. Kit's philosophy is that simple, text-based emails outperform heavily designed ones -- and the data backs this up. If your business relies on building personal relationships through email, Kit is purpose-built for that.

  • Best for: Creators, bloggers, newsletter-driven businesses, personal brands
  • Limitations: Limited design templates, e-commerce features are basic
  • Learning curve: Low -- the interface prioritizes simplicity

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) -- Best Value for Growing Lists

Free plan: 300 emails/day | Starter: $9/month | Business: $18/month

Brevo offers the best value for businesses with growing email lists. Unlike most competitors that charge per subscriber, Brevo charges per email sent. This means you can have 100,000 contacts on your list and only pay based on how many emails you actually send. The Starter plan at $9 per month includes 20,000 emails -- enough for a substantial operation. Brevo also includes SMS marketing, WhatsApp campaigns, and a built-in CRM at no extra cost.

  • Best for: Budget-conscious businesses with large lists, multi-channel marketing (email + SMS)
  • Limitations: Email builder is less polished than Mailchimp, deliverability requires proper setup
  • Learning curve: Low to moderate

Design & Media

Professional visuals are non-negotiable in 2026. Customers judge your business by how it looks. These no-code design tools produce professional results without requiring design training. For free alternatives, browse our collection of best free online tools for 2026 including image editors, generators, and more.

Canva -- Best for All-Purpose Design

Free plan available | Pro: $15/month | Teams: $10/user/month

Canva is the Swiss army knife of no-code design. Social media graphics, presentations, logos, flyers, business cards, video thumbnails, infographics, and even short videos. The template library is massive, and the drag-and-drop editor makes it possible for anyone to produce professional-looking visuals in minutes. The free plan is surprisingly generous, and the Pro plan at $15 per month unlocks background removal, brand kits, premium templates, and a content planner. Every founder should have Canva in their toolkit.

Figma -- Best for UI/UX Design and Prototyping

Free plan: 3 files | Professional: $15/editor/month | Organization: $45/editor/month

Figma is the industry standard for interface design and prototyping. If you are building a web app or mobile app with no-code tools, designing it in Figma first saves hours of iteration. The free plan allows three active files, which is enough for a single product. Figma's prototyping features let you create interactive mockups that look and feel like a real app, making it invaluable for testing ideas before building them in Bubble, Adalo, or FlutterFlow.

Descript -- Best for Video and Podcast Editing

Free plan available | Hobbyist: $24/month | Pro: $33/month

Descript lets you edit video and audio by editing text. It transcribes your content automatically, and you cut, rearrange, and edit by manipulating the transcript. This is genuinely revolutionary for non-editors. Need to remove an "um"? Delete the word from the transcript. Want to rearrange a section? Cut and paste the text. It also includes AI-powered features like filler word removal, studio sound enhancement, and eye contact correction. If video or podcasting is part of your content strategy, Descript makes editing accessible to anyone.

How to Choose Your No-Code Stack

The biggest mistake founders make with no-code tools is using too many of them. Every tool you add is another login to manage, another integration to maintain, and another monthly bill. Start with the minimum viable stack and add tools only when you hit a clear limitation.

If You Are Building a Content Business or Blog

Start with WordPress + Elementor or GitHub Pages for the website, Kit or Brevo for email, Canva for graphics, and Google Sheets for tracking. Total cost: $0 to $30 per month. This stack handles everything you need to publish content, grow an audience, and monetize through digital products or affiliates. Read our passive income ideas for 2026 for monetization strategies that pair perfectly with a content business.

If You Are Building a SaaS Product

Start with Bubble for the application, Stripe or LemonSqueezy for payments, Mailchimp or Kit for customer communication, and Make for backend automations. Total cost: $50 to $150 per month. This stack can take you from zero to thousands in monthly recurring revenue. Design your UI in Figma first, build in Bubble, and automate operations with Make.

If You Are Selling Digital Products

Start with Carrd or Framer for a landing page, Gumroad for sales and delivery, Kit for email marketing, and Canva for product design. Total cost: $0 to $50 per month. This is the leanest stack for digital product businesses. Our guide to reselling digital products shows you how to generate revenue even if you do not create the products yourself.

If You Are Building a Mobile App

Start with FlutterFlow or Adalo for the app, Supabase or Firebase for the backend, Stripe for payments, and Brevo for transactional emails. Total cost: $30 to $100 per month. Design in Figma, build in your app platform of choice, and use Make to handle backend workflows that connect your app to other services.

If You Are Building a Service Business

Start with Framer or Webflow for the website, Tally for intake forms, Notion for project management and client portals, Stripe for invoicing, and Mailchimp for client communication. Total cost: $0 to $50 per month. This stack handles lead capture, client onboarding, project delivery, invoicing, and follow-up.

Final Thoughts

The best no-code tools in 2026 are not just prototyping toys. They are legitimate business infrastructure that can take you from idea to revenue without writing a single line of code. The tools covered in this guide represent the current state of the art across every category: website builders, app builders, automation, databases, forms, payments, email, and design.

The key insight is this: the tool does not matter nearly as much as the execution. A mediocre business idea built on the most powerful no-code platform will still fail. A great business idea built on the simplest free tools can generate real revenue. Start with the leanest stack possible, focus on getting to your first paying customer, and add complexity only when simplicity becomes a bottleneck.
